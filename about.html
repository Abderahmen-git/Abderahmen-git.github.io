About

Defending infrastructure, from the SOC to the pipeline

A summary of who I am, how I got into cybersecurity, and where I'm headed next.

Professional summary

I'm a cybersecurity engineer from Tunisia with a blue-team core — SOC monitoring, incident response, and detection engineering — extended into DevSecOps and cloud-native security. I like the problems that sit at the intersection of the two: making sure the pipelines that ship software are as well-instrumented and defensible as the network they deploy into.

My work spans SIEM platforms (Wazuh, Splunk, QRadar), case management and threat intel tooling (TheHive, Cortex, MISP), and the DevSecOps stack that secures modern delivery: GitLab CI/CD, Docker, Kubernetes, Helm, ArgoCD, Vault, Trivy, and SonarQube. I'm comfortable on both Linux and Windows, and write Python and Bash to automate the repetitive parts of the job.

Career objective

I'm looking for a SOC, detection engineering, or DevSecOps role where I can keep building detection content and hardening delivery pipelines at organizations that take security seriously — the kind of team that treats a false positive as a tuning problem, not a shrug.
